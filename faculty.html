Faculty: Accountability & Transparency

Simulator Students Faculty Templates

Core principle

If AI affects grades, feedback, or opportunity, human accountability must remain central.

Lower-risk uses (with disclosure)

  • Drafting announcements/emails
  • Generating examples and practice questions
  • Drafting rubric-aligned feedback (as a draft)
  • Alternate explanations (plain language)
  • Accessibility improvements (structure, headings, alt text suggestions)

Higher-stakes uses (require safeguards)

  • Automated grading without review
  • High-impact decisions based on AI output
  • Uploading student work to tools with unclear retention
  • Using “AI detectors” as proof of misconduct

Safeguards that matter

  • Human-in-the-loop review
  • Rubric anchoring (require rubric references)
  • Spot-check verification
  • Clear student disclosure
  • Accessibility checks (readability, formats)

Use AI to support your thinking, not replace it

Responsible AI use helps you learn more effectively while avoiding academic integrity and misinformation risks.

Responsible uses

  • Brainstorming and outlining
  • Requesting explanations and examples
  • Improving clarity and structure of your own work
  • Debugging code (and explaining the fix in your own words)
  • Study guides (verify with course materials)

Higher-risk uses

  • Submitting AI output as original work
  • Copy/pasting answers without understanding
  • Fabricated citations/sources
  • Using AI during assessments when prohibited
  • Sharing private or sensitive data in prompts

Quick self-check

  • Can I explain the result without AI?
  • Did I verify key facts with reliable sources or course materials?
  • Did I follow my course’s disclosure/citation rules?
  • Did I avoid sharing private information?
